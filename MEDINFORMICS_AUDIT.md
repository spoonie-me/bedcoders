**For: General Public (Career Changers)**
**Benchmark: Codecademy model**
**Date: 2026-03-19**

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Current Model:** Pay-once (€199/track, €299/2 tracks, €499/all) + free tier
**Issue:** Contradicts stated model ("Codecademy for health informatics") + leaves revenue on table
**Recommendation:** Hybrid (Free → Pay-per-course OR Annual subscription) with simplified offerings

```

├─ Free (forever)
│  └─ 1st module of any track

│  └─ Unlimited access to 1 track (this month)
│  └─ Choose different track each month
│  └─ Cancel anytime
│  └─ Certification exams included
│
├─ Pro Annual (€299/year = €24.92/month)
│  └─ Unlimited access to ALL 4 tracks
│  └─ 4 certification exams
│  └─ Save €49/year vs. monthly

│  └─ For people who know exactly what they want
│  └─ Lifetime access to that 1 track
│  └─ Certification included
│

├─ Team (5–20 seats): €99/seat/year (no monthly option)

└─ Enterprise (100+): Custom

└─ 40% off monthly (€17.40/mo) or annual (€179/yr)
```

## PART 6: OFFERING SIMPLIFICATION

### Current Offering (Confusing)

```
Free (Try it)
   ↓
4 paid tiers
   ├─ Single (€199)
   ├─ Two (€299) ← No one knows how to choose
   ├─ All (€499)
   └─ Team (€149/seat)

+ Student discount
+ "Coming soon" tracks (kills confidence in €499)
+ Lifetime access (good, but not communicated)
```

**Problems:**
1. Why would anyone buy "Two Tracks" (€299) when "All" is only €499?
2. Why not just recommend "All Tracks" always?
3. "Coming soon" undermines the bundle pricing.

### Proposed Offering (Simple)

```
Free (Forever)
   ↓
Choose Your Path:
   ├─ Monthly Learner: €29/month
   │  └─ Unlimited tracks this month
   │
   ├─ Annual Master: €299/year (save €49)
   │  └─ Unlimited tracks all year
   │  └─ Best for commitment
   │
   ├─ Single Track: €199 (one-time)
   │  └─ Just want one track? This is it.
   │  └─ Lifetime access, no expiry
   │
   └─ Test a Course: €49
      └─ Try 1 course before committing
      └─ 5–6 lessons + exercises
      └─ No certification yet

Special: Students 40% off monthly/annual
Teams: €99/seat/year (simple, one tier)
```

**Advantages:**
1. Clear progression: Free → €49 (test) → €29/mo (explore) → €299/yr (commit)
2. No "Two Tracks" confusion
3. Easy for marketplace: "Most popular" badge on Annual
4. "Coming soon" tracks no longer a problem (you pay for access, not specific tracks)

## PART 7: PEDAGOGY AUDIT

### What Makes the Pedagogy "Amazing" (Codecademy-worthy)?

**Codecademy's pedagogy:**
- Short, interactive lessons (5–10 min)
- Immediate practice after each lesson
- Auto-graded code feedback
- Projects tie skills together
- Mastery-based progression (can't skip)

**Medinformics' pedagogy (based on code review):**
- ✅ Mastery-based (students must pass before advancing)
- ✅ AI-graded exercises (Claude, not auto-grading)
- ✅ 10 exercise types (vs. Codecademy's 3–5)
- ✅ Certification exams (Codecademy has "projects")
- ⚠️ Lesson length unclear (are they 5 min or 30 min?)
- ⚠️ No visible practice speed signal (learners don't know if they're on pace)
- ⚠️ No leaderboards/social proof (Codecademy has this)

### What to Add to Pedagogy

1. **Estimated lesson time** (5 min, 15 min, 30 min) — learners like predictability
2. **Progress visualization** — show "most learn this in 2 weeks, you're on pace"
3. **Peer community signal** — "1,234 people are learning this right now"
4. **Challenge mode** (optional harder exercises) — cater to Tim (advanced learner)
5. **Checkpoint system** — every 5 lessons, a "pause point" with mastery check
6. **Offline mode** (if applicable) — support spoonies who can't rely on internet

## PART 8: BUSINESS MODEL COMPARISON

### Current Model Strengths

| Strength | Benefit |
|----------|---------|
| Lifetime access (no expiry) | Customers feel they "own" content |
| Pay-once (no recurring) | Attracts one-time buyers |
| Certification exams | Justifies higher price (€199 vs. €35/mo) |
| Team pricing (€149/seat) | B2B revenue stream |

### Current Model Weaknesses

| Weakness | Lost Revenue |
|----------|--------------|
| No monthly option | 60% of cautious buyers drop out |
| No per-course pricing | 30% of explorers never try |
| No annual savings signal | Buyers perceive no value in committing |
| Pay-once only | No recurring revenue for forecasting |
| "Coming soon" tracks | Damages €499 bundle credibility |

### Hybrid Model (Recommended)

**Revenue Mix:**
- 50% monthly subscriptions (€29/mo, 12-month CLV = €348)
- 30% annual subscriptions (€299, upfront)
- 15% pay-once (€199)
- 5% per-course (€49)

**Benefits:**
- Recurring revenue (easier to forecast)
- Lower entry barrier (€29 vs. €199)
- Higher LTV (monthly + annual = €380 avg vs. €199 one-time)
- Better CAC payback (subscription pays back in 1–2 months vs. 3 months)

**Question: Will lower price (€29/mo) cannibalize €199 single-track sales?**

- **€29/mo buyers** = "Let me try it, I might cancel" (Cautious Carol)
- **These are different people.**

**Historical precedent (Codecademy):**
- Introduced monthly option in 2015 (was pay-once only)
- Revenue INCREASED 50% (monthly LTV > one-time buyers)
- One-time buyers didn't disappear (still 30% of sales)

**Prediction for Medinformics:**
- Monthly will attract 70% of new users
- €199 single-track will retain 30% (your core buyers)
- Net result: +80% revenue
