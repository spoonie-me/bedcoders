About

Making the invisible
visible through language.

Bed Coders was built because the people who most need economic retraining are the ones most locked out of the systems designed to provide it.

The mission is simple: give people with chronic illness, disability, and conditions that keep them housebound access to real technical skills — taught in a way that works with a body like theirs, not against it. No streaks. No performance. No inspiration. Just learning that fits the life you actually have.

Bed Coders is built and run by Roi Shternin — keynote speaker, author, organizational consultant, and patient advocate. He has spent years working at the intersection of illness, language, and systems change: training over 20,000 healthcare professionals across 27 countries, authoring seven books, and founding patient advocacy organisations including the Israeli Society for Dysautonomia and The Patient School.

He served as the world's first Patient in Residence at the Ludwig Boltzmann Institute in Vienna, and delivered a TEDx talk titled "Revolution from My Bed" — long before Bed Coders existed as a platform, but not before it existed as an idea. He holds an MA in International Health & Social Management from MCI Innsbruck, teaches patient communications at IMC Krems, and is completing his Lebens- und Sozialberatung (LSB) counsellor certification in 2026.

The core thread running through all of it — the speaking, the writing, the advocacy, the teaching — is a single conviction: that the people who live closest to limitation often understand systems most clearly, and that making the invisible visible is how things change.

Bed Coders is the most direct expression of that conviction he has built yet. The scholarship model — patients funding patients, no gatekeepers — is not a feature. It's the point.
